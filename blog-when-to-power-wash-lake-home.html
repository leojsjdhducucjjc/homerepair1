When to Power Wash a Dock, Deck, or Siding

Exterior surfaces around the lake need regular cleaning to stay safe and look their best.

Power washing is one of the fastest ways to improve the look of a property, but timing matters. Around Lake of the Ozarks, buildup from pollen, moisture, algae, and lake traffic can make surfaces dingy faster than many homeowners expect.

Spring Is a Strong Starting Point

Many owners schedule exterior washing before the busy season starts. This helps docks, patios, decks, and siding look cleaner right as guests or family begin using the property more often.

Before Painting or Staining

Cleaning is also important before exterior refinishing. Dirt, mildew, and loose debris can keep coatings from bonding the way they should.

When Safety Becomes a Concern

If a dock, walkway, or deck feels slick, it is a good time to inspect for buildup. Algae and grime can make these surfaces more dangerous, especially near the water.

After Heavy Seasonal Use

Vacation rentals and lake homes often collect extra debris during peak months. A mid-season or end-of-season cleaning can help protect surfaces and keep the property looking maintained.
